IBM





IBM stands for International Business Machine and is currently the biggest IT service company in the world with over 300 offices and 2,82,100 employees working in more than 177 nations of the world.

The American company founded by financial capitalist Charles Ranlett Flint on the 16th of June 1911 and has its headquarters at Armonk, New York, United States. IBM’s revenue generation today is achieved through its five sectors: Cloud & Cognitive Software, Global Technology Services (GTS), Global Business Services (GBS), Global Financing, and Systems.

Its GTS division takes care of the best IT services for its customers all over the world. These services include process outsourcing and maintenance of very important client systems and infrastructure. Its GBS division is entrusted with consultancy, project management solutions, and system integration. IBM shares sold at over $135 per share and sustained a market capitalization of over US $122 billion as of April 2022. Currently, the company has three major individual shareholders: James M. Whitehurst (148,608 shares), the CEO– Arvind Krishna (86,188 shares), and James J. Kavanaugh (77,514 shares).

In recent times, well over 92 percent of the world’s top 100 banks require the services of IBM because of their dependence on mainframes for security and uninterrupted and efficient processing of financial transactions. Looking to the future, IBM is ideally positioned to assist customers and consumers in using AI and data analytics to develop smarter enterprises. IBM has actively invested in technology and its employees in recent years, but top management is also examining IBM's presence in key high-value areas of the IT industry such as AI, blockchain, cloud computing, and information security. IBM is ranked second in the Top IT Companies in the World 202 IBM also offers blockchain solutions that may help people to develop their own cryptocurrency exchange.
