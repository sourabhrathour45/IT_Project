Accenture





Accenture by far has proven to be a major player in the tech world and also one of the biggest IT companies in the world and finest software companies in the world.

In 1989, Accenture plc, formerly known as Anderson Consulting, split from the accounting firm Arthur Anderson– a firm for which it was formerly the business and technology consulting division. The two companies broke all contractual ties in 2001.

Accenture Is a well-recognized global professional service and management consulting firm that provides the best IT services, consulting, digital marketing, operations solutions, business process outsourcing, IT, electronic, and cloud services. The American-Irish multinational company is split into several units to facilitate its many services and operations, and for efficient management. Those units include:
Accenture Consulting is responsible for business, management, and technology consulting.

Accenture Strategy: This unit takes care of the company’s tech, operations, and business strategy services. Accenture Operations is another unit of the company saddled with the responsibility of outsourcing business processes, IT services, security, and managed operations. Accenture Technology: The operations unit is concerned with software research and development, administration, and execution. Aside from all the very interesting things you now know about this top IT company, there’s one thing that’s even more marvelous — its workforce population. Accenture had about 6,99,000 individuals employed in over 120 countries around the globe. To further assure you of this firm’s credibility, know that it has big brands like Unilever, the Royal Shakespeare Company, and Marriott as its clients.
